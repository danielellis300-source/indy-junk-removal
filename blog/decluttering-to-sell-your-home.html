Preparing Your Indianapolis Home to Sell: Decluttering for Showings

June 25, 2026 6 min read Indianapolis Junk Removal Co

Real estate agents consistently point to decluttering as one of the highest-return, lowest-cost things a seller can do before listing. Buyers are trying to picture their own life in a space, and a home full of someone else's belongings makes that harder — which shows up directly in how fast a home sells and for how much.

Why Clutter Hurts Showings

Cluttered rooms photograph smaller than they actually are, and listing photos are how most buyers decide whether to even schedule a showing. A cramped-looking photo can cost you a showing before a buyer ever sets foot in the house.

Room-by-Room Priorities

  • Living areas: Remove excess furniture so the room's actual size and flow are visible — a room that looks cramped in photos gets scrolled past
  • Kitchen counters: Clear everything except one or two intentional items — buyers evaluate counter space directly
  • Closets: Buyers open closets specifically to judge storage; a packed closet reads as "not enough storage" even if the closet itself is a good size
  • Garage: A cluttered garage undersells one of the most valuable spaces in the home for many buyers
  • Basement: Especially important if finished square footage is part of your listing's value proposition

Personal Items vs. Excess Items

There's a difference between removing personal photos and knick-knacks (a staging choice) and removing genuine excess furniture and clutter (a decluttering necessity). Both matter, but the second one has a much bigger impact on how spacious and move-in-ready a home feels.

Timing Relative to Listing Date

Plan decluttering to finish at least a few days before listing photos are taken — rushing it the morning of a photo shoot rarely produces the same result as having the space genuinely settled and clean beforehand.

What to Do With Everything You Remove

Excess furniture and belongings generally go one of three places: a storage unit if you're keeping items for the new home, donation if you're parting with them, or haul-away if it's simply excess you won't need again. For a full-house declutter ahead of a sale, a single scheduled pickup is usually far faster than trying to fit everything into a storage unit run yourself.
