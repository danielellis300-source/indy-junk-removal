Mattress Disposal in Indianapolis: Rules, Recycling & Removal Options

March 5, 2026 5 min read Indianapolis Junk Removal Co

Mattresses cause more disposal headaches than almost any other single household item. They're bulky, hard to fold down, and many landfills and haulers restrict them unless they're wrapped — for good reason: loose mattresses are notorious for jamming compaction equipment at transfer stations.

Why Mattresses Are Restricted

The springs and dense foam inside a mattress can tangle in compactor machinery, which is why many facilities require mattresses to be bagged in plastic before drop-off, and some municipal bulk pickup programs cap how many can be collected per address or per year.

Recycling Is the Better Option

A mattress is roughly 90% recyclable by weight — steel springs, foam, and wood framing can all be separated and reused. Specialized mattress recycling facilities break units down for material recovery rather than landfilling them whole, which is a meaningfully better environmental outcome than curbside disposal.

Your Options in Indianapolis

  • Retailer take-back — many mattress retailers will remove your old mattress when delivering a new one, sometimes for a small fee
  • Municipal bulk pickup — available but limited per household and requires the mattress to meet size/wrapping requirements
  • Donation — only an option if the mattress is recent, clean, and free of stains or odor; most charities won't take older mattresses due to hygiene concerns and resale restrictions in many states
  • Junk removal service — picks up from any room in the house (including hauling down stairs, which is its own physical challenge) and routes it to proper disposal or recycling

What Not to Do

Leaving a mattress at the curb without checking your municipality's specific rules can result in it simply not being collected — or in a fine. Loose (unwrapped) mattresses are the most common reason a bulk pickup item gets left behind by the crew.
