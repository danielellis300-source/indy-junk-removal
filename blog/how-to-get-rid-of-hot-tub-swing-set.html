How to Get Rid of an Old Hot Tub, Swing Set, or Playset

May 28, 2026 5 min read Indianapolis Junk Removal Co

Backyard structures are a category of their own. They're not designed to be moved once installed, which means removing one usually involves disassembly, not just hauling — and that changes what the job actually requires compared to a typical junk pickup.

Hot Tubs

Hot tubs are deceptively heavy even when empty, and most are built with a wood or composite frame that has to be cut apart or disassembled rather than lifted whole — very few will fit through a standard gate intact. Drain and disconnect any electrical or plumbing connections before removal day; a licensed electrician should handle a hardwired 240V connection if that's how yours is set up.

Swing Sets and Playsets

Wooden playsets are usually bolted together in sections and need to be broken down piece by piece — a full disassembly of a large wooden set can take a couple of hours even for an experienced crew. Metal swing sets are typically lighter and faster to break down, but concrete footings (common on older installations) add real time and effort to remove.

Trampolines

Fast to disassemble — most trampolines break down to frame, springs, and mat in under 30 minutes. The main challenge is size once it's out; a 14-foot frame doesn't fold small and needs a truck with enough bed space.

Sheds

Small prefab sheds can sometimes be removed largely intact depending on size and access; larger or built-in-place sheds typically need to be deconstructed. Check the foundation type too — sheds on concrete pads leave that pad behind unless removal is specifically arranged.

What This Means for Pricing

Backyard structure removal is priced with labor and disassembly time factored in, not just truck space — which is why these jobs often cost more than their size alone would suggest. Getting a photo-based quote in advance helps set accurate expectations before the crew arrives.

Before You Schedule

Clear a path from the structure to where the truck will park, and disconnect any utilities (power, water) in advance if you're able to safely do so. This alone can meaningfully shorten the job.
