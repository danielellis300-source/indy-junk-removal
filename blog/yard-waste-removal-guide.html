Yard Waste Removal Guide: Branches, Brush & Storm Cleanup in Indianapolis

June 18, 2026 5 min read Indianapolis Junk Removal Co

Indiana's weather is hard on trees and landscaping — spring storms, summer wind events, and winter ice all generate yard debris on their own schedule, separate from routine seasonal cleanup. Knowing what your regular yard waste program covers versus what needs separate handling saves a lot of frustration.

What Routine Yard Waste Pickup Typically Covers

  • Grass clippings and leaves, usually bagged
  • Small branches, often with a diameter and length limit
  • Garden debris and trimmings

What It Usually Doesn't Cover

  • Large branches and limbs — anything beyond a program's diameter limit typically needs a separate hauler
  • Tree stumps — almost never accepted through routine yard waste programs; these need dedicated removal or grinding
  • Storm debris in bulk — a single storm can generate more brush than weeks of a normal program's capacity handles
  • Dirt, sod, and mulch removed during landscaping — heavy and typically excluded from standard yard waste pickup
  • Fencing, sheds, or structural debris mixed in with yard cleanup

After a Storm

Following a significant storm, yard waste volume can overwhelm routine collection schedules for weeks. If a downed tree or major limb has damaged a fence, deck, or structure, document it for insurance before anything is moved — this matters more than people realize when filing a claim later.

Landscaping Projects

Removing an overgrown hedge row, an old garden bed, or preparing a yard for renovation typically produces more volume and heavier material (root balls, sod, soil) than routine yard waste handles. Plan for a dedicated pickup rather than assuming the weekly program will absorb it.

Tree Stump Removal Note

Stump grinding and stump removal are different services — grinding leaves the root system in the ground and chips the visible stump, while full removal extracts the root ball. Junk removal typically handles hauling debris after either process, not the grinding/extraction itself, which is usually a separate tree service call.
