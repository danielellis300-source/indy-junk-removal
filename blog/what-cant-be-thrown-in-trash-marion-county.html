What Can't Be Thrown in the Trash in Marion County, Indiana?

January 29, 2026 6 min read Indianapolis Junk Removal Co

Indianapolis's curbside collection (run through the Department of Public Works for Marion County residents) accepts standard household trash, but a surprising number of common items are restricted, require special scheduling, or are outright banned from the regular truck. Putting the wrong thing at the curb can mean it just sits there — or gets a hefty tag from your hauler.

Items Marion County Curbside Trash Will NOT Take

  • Household hazardous waste — paint, solvents, pesticides, motor oil, car batteries, propane tanks
  • Electronics — Indiana law (the Indiana E-Waste Program) restricts landfilling of most electronics; TVs, monitors, and computers need a dedicated recycler
  • Tires — never accepted curbside; most tire shops charge a small disposal fee per tire
  • Construction and demolition debris in large quantities — drywall, lumber, roofing, concrete typically require a separate hauler or dumpster
  • Large appliances with refrigerant — refrigerators, freezers, and window AC units need refrigerant recovery before disposal (this is federally regulated under the Clean Air Act, not just a local rule)
  • Yard waste in some circumstances — large branches and stumps often exceed size and weight limits for regular yard waste pickup

Marion County's Large-Item Pickup Program

Indianapolis residents on the city's curbside service do get scheduled large-item pickups for bulky household items, but there are real limits: item caps per pickup, restrictions on what qualifies, and scheduling that can be weeks out — which doesn't help if you're clearing a house for a sale or move this week. We cover exactly how this compares to hiring a crew in Bulk Trash Pickup vs. Professional Junk Removal.

Where Restricted Items Actually Go

  • Household hazardous waste: Marion County operates household hazardous waste collection events and a permanent drop-off; check the current schedule before loading up your trunk with old paint cans.
  • Electronics: Certified e-waste recyclers accept TVs and computers, sometimes for a small fee on CRT units. More detail in Electronics Recycling in Indianapolis.
  • Tires: Most tire retailers will take old tires when you buy new ones, often for $2–5 each.
  • Appliances: Many scrap metal recyclers take appliances for free or even pay a small amount, since they're mostly recyclable steel — but they still need refrigerant removed first.
