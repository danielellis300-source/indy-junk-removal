Appliance Disposal 101: How to Get Rid of Old Refrigerators, Washers & Dryers

February 26, 2026 6 min read Indianapolis Junk Removal Co

Old appliances feel simple to get rid of — until you try. They're heavy, awkward to move, and several types are legally restricted from regular disposal because of what's inside them. Here's what's actually required and what your realistic options are.

Refrigerators, Freezers & Window AC Units

These contain refrigerant, which is federally regulated under the Clean Air Act and cannot legally be vented into the atmosphere. Before one of these appliances can be scrapped or landfilled, the refrigerant has to be professionally recovered by someone certified to do it — this isn't a local quirk, it's federal law. Most scrap yards and appliance recyclers have this capability built into their process, which is why routing through a proper recycler (rather than just setting it at the curb) is the compliant path.

Washers, Dryers & Ovens

No refrigerant concerns here, which makes these the easiest major appliances to dispose of. They're mostly steel, so scrap metal recyclers often take them for free — sometimes even pay a small amount depending on current scrap prices.

Dishwashers & Microwaves

Straightforward recycling candidates. Curbside large-item pickup programs will sometimes take these, though scheduling can run weeks out.

The Weight and Access Problem

Beyond the legal side, the practical problem with appliances is simply moving them. A full-size refrigerator can weigh 200–300 lbs, and getting one down a flight of stairs or through a narrow doorway without damaging walls or floors takes technique most people don't have on hand. This is the number-one reason homeowners call for help with appliances specifically, even when they're comfortable hauling everything else themselves.

Your Realistic Options

  • Retailer haul-away — many appliance stores will remove your old unit when delivering a new one, often for a fee or sometimes free
  • Scrap metal recyclers — accept most appliances, may pay for units that are mostly steel
  • Municipal large-item pickup — available but scheduled and limited; see our bulk pickup comparison
  • Junk removal service — handles the lifting, stairs, and legal disposal in one trip, same day
